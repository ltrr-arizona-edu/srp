LINKS TO BACKGROUND INFORMATION
ON TREE-RINGS,  DROUGHT & CLIMATE VARIABILITY

Tree-Ring Basics:
  • Tree-Ring Basics  - a short tutorial from the Laboratory of Tree-Ring Research (LTRR); includes an interactive training module on how to plot and cross-date tree-ring cores
     
  • The Ultimate Tree-Ring Web Pages (by LTRR Alumnus Henri D. Grissino-Mayer) contains information on all aspects of dendrochronology and is an excellent first reference for beginners through career dendrochronologists. Some frequently visited Ultimate pages are:
    • Definitions and Principles of Dendrochronology
    • A - Z Index of the Ultimate Tree-Ring Web Pages
       
  • Glossary of Dendrochronology

Information on Drought & Climate Variability:

  • North American Drought:  A Paleo Perspective -- tutorial from the NOAA Paleoclimatology Program
     
  • Atlas of Reconstructions of Past North American Droughts -  includes times series of PDSI for individual grid points, based on tree rings
     
  • NCDC Climate Monitoring:  see Drought Monitoring Link
     
  • CLIMAS Climate Assessment for the Southwest site
     
    • Reconstructing Past Climate in the Southwest - tree-ring based reconstructions of cool-season precipitation for climatic divisions in Arizona & New Mexico
    • Online tool for access to reconstructed precipitation data by climatic division
    • Drought and Climate in Arizona: Top Ten Questions and Answers [pdf]
      This 23-page report outlines several questions frequently asked of CLIMAS researchers about Arizona's longterm drought and climate.
       
  • An assessment of the recent drought in northern Arizona: A comparison of paleo and instrumental data  - webpage summary by LTRR researchers comparing the recent drought years with the paleorecord of reconstructed precipitation
